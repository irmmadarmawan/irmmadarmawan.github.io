About Me

Hi, I’m Irmma Darmawan, but you can call me Irmma!
I am currently 16 years old and living in Jakarta. So long
stroy short, I started to learn ballet when I was 4 years old.
By that being said, I have been learning ballet almost for
13 years. Yes, I survived :) Not only focusing on ballet,
I have also been learning jazz dance style......

About Me

Ballet and jazz have a very diffrent styles and characteristics. But, if I have a pick a favorite… it would be ballet. I have been joining dance classes in Namarina Dance Academy, a ballet, jazz, and fitness school in Jakarta. Since March 2020, I have been given the opportunity to join Namarina Dance Academy’s semi-professional dance company called Namarina Youth Dance, as an apprentice. WIth a long period time of dancing, I have gained precious experiences and life lessons. So, enjoy my blog and I hope it could give new perspectives and information about dance itself.

Why Ballet?

When I was still around 8 to 10 years old, I considered ballet
as my escape place from the reality, such as school and my
responsibilities. But as time goes by, I see dancing not only as
my escape place, but also as something that I am passionate
of. I love how I could express my feelings through my
movements that create a beautiful piece of art. However,....

Why Ballet?

It isn’t always been easy in my dancing journey. I have faced many challenges that I need to tackle along the way. But it is not that easy to make me quit dancing. I braved and suspended myself in facing all the challenges. I want to nail every movement, especially more advanced techniques. The fighting spirit inside of me is making me continue to achieve my goals in dancing. What is my favourite part of being a dancer? Maybe you could know already, but of all things, my favourite one would be the chance to perform and dance on stage theatre. It always feels so magical. Being on the stage with all the spotlights focused on us is terrifying yet exciting at the same time. All the worries are gone when I step my foot on the stage. Lastly, it felt like a dream when I step out from the stage after performing.
